Mission
Nelson’s Film organizes recurring, accessible film screenings in neighborhoods where intercultural encounters do not always happen naturally. Professionally organized and locally supported, we bring residents together to experience the same film in their own language. In this way, lasting connections grow step-by-step in an ever-changing neighborhood. Watching together, Living together
Vision
Nelson’s Film contributes to social cohesion in Dutch neighborhoods. We believe that when people laugh together or are touched by the same images, it becomes visible that they are more alike than they are different. We show that connection is not just found in words. Our ambition is to organize connecting film evenings throughout the Netherlands that are professionally organized and locally supported.
Core Values
Connection without words Watching together connects people. A smile or a look of recognition can be more powerful than dialogue.
Accessibility (Inclusive) Everyone in the neighborhood is welcome, regardless of language or background. Film tickets can optionally be purchased afterwards.
Local ownership Professionally organized, but supported by residents and partners in the neighborhood.
Sustainable professionalism Not a one-time event, but a recurring concept that is organizationally strong and maintains a long-term presence (e.g., once a month throughout the entire summer, year after year).
Neighborhood-oriented and dynamic The programming (and languages) moves along with the ever-changing composition of the neighborhood.

Target Groups
Primary:
Municipalities: Neighborhood directors, Area connectors, Neighborhood managers
Community centers, social brokers
(Cultural) foundations
Cultural breeding grounds (broedplaatsen)
Secondary:
Residents (collectives)
Local entrepreneurs
Each target group has its own advantages and disadvantages. Resident collectives and foundations can often switch gears much faster; there is more energy and decisiveness. Challenges often lie on the financial side. Appeals will have to be made for subsidies they often already use for other initiatives. Support, time, and expertise from Nelson’s Film are required. Municipalities will be more inclined to agree to the amount (which is relatively low) but can be stiff and hesitant regarding execution. In practice, both parties will often be needed. An approach starting from the "energy" (foundation and residents) is desirable for faster scaling.
Positioning
Nelson’s Film is a recurring community concept that uses film as a tool for connection.
We are:
Professionally organized – but with a "cozy" (kneuterig) neighborhood feel
Locally supported
Sustainably present
Neighborhood-oriented and dynamic
We are not a commercial event organization. We are a foundation that enables sustainable scaling without losing the character of a grassroots resident initiative. Every screening is organized with and for the neighborhood. Local entrepreneurs and initiatives are actively involved. This creates a network around the film evening that reaches further than the screen. We unburden where we can, but at the same time, we have an eye and space for local input and expertise. For us, film is not an end in itself. It is a means to bring people closer together in an accessible way.
What we definitely are NOT:
A high-end cultural organization
A commercial film organization
A poorly organized, dull neighborhood initiative
A flashy film company
Brand story / Pitch
It all started very simply. Two sheets sewn together on a football fence, a rented projector, and neighbors who carried their own sofa outside to enjoy a film together under the stars. The foundation of Nelson’s Film was born: a low-threshold evening that was all about pure fun. But soon we saw a sharp contrast. While the neighborhood around us changed into a mix of cultures and backgrounds, our audience remained a reflection of our own familiar bubble.
We noticed that the unknown often remains unloved. Starting a conversation with someone who stands far outside your own world can be uncomfortable or scary. We see that real connection does not necessarily have to be in words. Especially considering we don’t even speak the same language! When you laugh together at the same joke or are touched by the same image, differences fall away. Film is the universal language that brings people closer together. To make that connection accessible to everyone, we facilitate the complex multilingualism involved. We ensure that language is not a barrier, but a bridge, so that everyone can experience the same film in their own language.
Yet that connection does not happen by itself. We saw beautiful neighborhood initiatives fail because the burden was carried by enthusiastic volunteers who simply lacked the time and expertise to keep it going. Connecting cultures requires more than just a good idea. It requires a script that works, continuity, and professional direction. It requires integrating learning experiences from across the country into a concept that gets stronger every time.
That is why Nelson’s Film has evolved. In the film world, they say the best direction is the direction you don't see. You get lost in the story and feel the emotion without thinking about the technique or the logistics behind the scenes. That is exactly our role in the neighborhood. We are the invisible director who takes care of the complex organization, the film rights, and the multilingual communication.
We build the stage and guard the structure so that the local stars, the residents and entrepreneurs, can shine in their own neighborhood. In this way, we turn a one-off event into a lasting tradition with maximum impact and minimal burden on the neighborhood. We handle the direction behind the scenes so the neighborhood can play the leading role in its own story of connection, month after month.
Nelson’s Film: Watching together, living together.
In short (for web)
Nelson’s Film began with two sheets on a fence and the simple desire to truly connect neighbors with one another. Because the unknown often remains unloved, we use the power of film and multilingualism to remove the barriers between cultures. We believe that lasting connection requires professional expertise and continuity, ensuring a neighborhood initiative doesn't burn out but actually flourishes.
 In the film world, the best direction is the direction you don't see, and that is exactly our role: we handle the technology, rights, and organization behind the scenes. In this way, we turn film nights into a valuable tradition where the neighborhood and its local STARS play the leading role every single month.

Tone of Voice
Nelson’s Film balances warmth and professionalism. The tone differs slightly per target group, but the core remains the same: clear, human, and reliable.
Towards residents:
Characteristics:
Warm
Accessible
Understandable (B1 level communication as much as possible)
Inviting
Likable
What we do:
Short sentences
Clear language
Emphasis on experiencing together
Positive but calm tone
Empathetic
What we don’t do:
Loud/Screamy
Over-enthusiastic
Commercial
Activist
Patronizing
Example: Watch a beautiful film in your own language together with your neighbors. Every month in your own neighborhood. Create your own film experience and bring your own blanket and drink. Not: The best movie night ever! You really can't miss this!
Towards municipalities:
Characteristics:
Professional
Concrete
Business-like but human
Official, but not "woolly" (vague)
What we do:
Mention structural impact
Speak about social cohesion
Clear formulations
Avoid jargon
What we don’t do:
Policy prose
Long official sentences
Marketing language
Emotional exaggeration
Example: Nelson’s Film organizes monthly film screenings that stimulate encounters between groups of residents and contribute to social cohesion in the neighborhood. Not: We realize impactful socio-cultural interventions within complex urban contexts.
